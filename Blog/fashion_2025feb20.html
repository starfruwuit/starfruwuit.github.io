It's hard to be fashionable in the throes of gender dysphoria, but it's worth it.

Let me explain. When you're trying to find clothes that don't make you want to disown your physical form and become a swarm of bees instead, there's a phase at the beginning where you're going to look really bad. In my case: when I came out for the first time in high school, I had a small rotation of thin cotton T-shirts from Goodfellow and wore the same pair of black knee-length shorts every day. I had a short haircut that was meant to make me look masc but only succeeded in emphasizing the roundness of my face.
